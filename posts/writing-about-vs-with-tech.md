Most people who call themselves technical writers are translators after the fact.
An engineer ships something, a PM writes a brief, and the writer arrives at the
end to turn it into a blog post or a doc. That works, but it caps what the writing
can do.

The better version of this job happens earlier — in the Slack thread where the
feature is still being argued about, in the call where the founder is explaining
the idea to an investor for the first time and it isn't landing yet. That's where
the real translation work is: not tech into English, but a half-formed idea into
a sentence someone can repeat to their own boss.
