§01 — About

I've spent five years being the person who understands both the product and the reader.

I'm a technical content writer and content strategist working mostly with early and growth-stage startups — the kind of companies where the product is genuinely hard to explain, and where bad explanations cost real deals. Over five-plus years, I've moved between technical documentation, ghostwriting, and long-form brand storytelling, usually for the same clients at different stages of their growth.

My work sits at the intersection of technical writing and content strategy: I don't just write the piece, I help decide what needs writing in the first place, who it's for, and what it has to do once it's published. That's meant developer documentation that actually reduces support load, LinkedIn ghostwriting that sounds like the founder on a good day, and case studies built to close deals rather than just look nice in a folder.

A lot of my client base sits in Web3 and AI — categories where the gap between what a product does and what a reader understands is often the biggest barrier to adoption, funding, or trust. I've written whitepaper-adjacent explainers, protocol documentation, and investor-facing narratives for teams who needed someone capable of sitting in a technical conversation and coming out the other side with something a general reader could follow.

The throughline across all of it is simplification without dumbing down — finding the true, specific version of a complex idea instead of a vague one.
